Dominant Conditions

Registered cases are dominated by depression-anxiety (62%) and psychotic disorders (23%). The true figure is likely much higher.

The Mind-Body Link

Anxiety was found in 91.36% of hypertension patients and 100% of diabetes patients, creating a negative cycle in health management.

Socio-Cultural Barriers

Stigma, poverty (22.3% of families are pre-prosperous), and low mental health literacy (55% don't know early signs) hinder help-seeking.

Service Landscape Challenges

Limited resources, infrastructure, and risk of misdiagnosis highlight the need for standardized and accurate assessment tools.

Research Questions

Guiding the investigation to bridge the identified gaps.

  • Diagnostic Accuracy

    How does the diagnostic accuracy (Sensitivity, Specificity, F1-Score, AUC) of the Venti chatbot compare to a clinical gold standard in identifying indicators of anxiety, depression, and suicide risk?

  • User Perception

    What are the user-perceived levels of usability (measured by CUQ) and trust (measured by S-TIAS) towards the Venti chatbot among patients with hypertension and diabetes?

  • Therapeutic Communication

    To what extent does the Venti chatbot employ therapeutic communication techniques, as defined by Stuart's Nurse-Patient Communication Model, in its interactions with users?

Systematic Review: AI for Mental Health Diagnosis (Yulianti et al., 2025)

Strong Diagnostic Performance

70.6% of studies showed good performance (AUC: 0.77–0.92). LLMs (GPT-4, BERT) significantly outperformed traditional methods, with AUC > 0.90 in depression detection.

Cultural Adaptation Challenges

Mixed to poor performance (29.4%) was found in non-English contexts (e.g., Bahasa Malaysia F1=0.66) and with multimodal models, highlighting generalization challenges.

Ethical & Methodological Gaps

Data privacy was underreported (70.6%), algorithmic bias persists, and 82.4% of studies are from high-income countries, limiting generalizability to low-resource settings.

Implications & Recommendations

NLP has great potential, but successful integration requires robust external validation, diverse datasets, and rigorous Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) to ensure relevance and equity.

Methodology

Data Analysis & Population

Data Analysis

Data analysis will be conducted through three complementary approaches. All data will be anonymized, using R Studio for quantitative analysis and NVivo for qualitative analysis.

Pillar 1: Diagnostic Accuracy Analysis (AI vs. Clinical Experts)

  • Focuses on objectively evaluating the AI chatbot's clinical performance.
  • A gold standard will be established by two clinical experts independently analyzing transcripts using a "Clinical Indicator Checklist."
  • Inter-rater reliability will be measured using Fleiss' Kappa to ensure consistency.
  • The AI's binary detection for each variable will be compared against the consensus gold standard.
  • Performance metrics will be calculated, including Sensitivity, Specificity, Precision, F1-Score, and AUC.

Pillar 2: User Perception Analysis (Quantitative)

  • Aims to measure the subjective perception and experience of participants.
  • After interaction, participants will complete the Chatbot Usability Questionnaire (CUQ) and the Short Trust in Automation Scale (S-TIAS).
  • Collected data will be analyzed using descriptive statistics (mean, standard deviation, frequency).
  • This provides a quantitative overview of usability and trust.

Pillar 3: Chatbot Therapeutic Communication Analysis (Qualitative)

  • Analyzes the chatbot's own behavior from a nursing theory perspective.
  • A qualitative analysis of transcripts will be conducted using Stuart's Nurse-Patient Therapeutic Communication Model as a framework.
  • The researcher will systematically identify, code, and count the frequency of therapeutic techniques (e.g., validation, clarification, reflection).
  • The analysis will also assess the relevance and appropriateness of the techniques used in response to participant statements.

Population & Sampling Method

Location & Technique

The study will be conducted in the community fostered by FIK UI & the Public Health Center in Batutulis, Bogor, using Convenience Sampling.

Target Population & Limitations

Adults (21-59 years) with Diabetes Mellitus (DM), Hypertension (HT), or both. The study does not include other chronic diseases like cancer.

Theoretical Frameworks

Underpinning the Concept of Compassionate Skepticism in AI

Hunt–Vitell Ethical Model (Ferrell & Ferrell, 2021)

Emphasizes moral awareness, judgment, and intent phases, adapted for AI to merge design empathy with rational control over machine decisions.

Trust as Reliance + "Extra Factor" (Duran & Formanek, 2021)

Defines trust in AI as a combination of system reliability (reliance) and moral commitment (extra factor), supporting "benefit of the doubt" and "cross-checking".

Ethical Framework (Ashok et al., 2022)

Presents a four-domain ontology (physical, cognitive, information, governance) to map and balance ethical implications like fairness, autonomy, and privacy.

AI Ethics & Governance Framework (WHO, 2021)

Uses information theory to evaluate AI transparency and emphasizes audit, validation, and human-in-the-loop as forms of systematic cross-checking.

Abstract Highlights

Background

Patients with chronic illness face elevated mental health risks; scalable, holistic tools are needed.

Objective

To develop and evaluate Venti, an AI chatbot grounded in nursing epistemology.

Outcomes

High diagnostic accuracy (AUC > 0.85), strong usability (CUQ), and rich thematic analysis of empathy.

Conclusion

Successfully validates a new nursing-centric AI model for scalable, ethical mental health screening.

Contribution

A validated, nursing-centric AI model for scalable, ethical mental health screening.

Anticipated Impact of the Study

Projecting the benefits for practice, science, and society.

Clinical Impact

Empowers nurses with an accurate screening tool, potentially reducing diagnostic errors and improving early detection. Increases patient access to preliminary mental health assessment, especially in resource-limited settings.

Scientific & Technological Impact

Provides a validated, nursing-informed AI model for a low-resource, non-English context. Contributes a unique dataset of Indonesian patient-chatbot interactions for future research on cultural and linguistic nuances in AI.

Community & Policy Impact

Offers a scalable solution to address gaps in community mental health services. The findings can inform public health policies on integrating AI tools to improve healthcare equity and reduce the burden on primary care facilities.
