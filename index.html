Systematic Review: AI for Mental Health Diagnosis (Yulianti et al., 2025)

Assessing the Potential and Pitfalls of NLP-Based Diagnostic Tools.

Strong Diagnostic Performance

70.6% of studies showed good performance (AUC: 0.77–0.92). LLMs (GPT-4, BERT) significantly outperformed traditional methods, with AUC > 0.90 in depression detection.

Cultural Adaptation Challenges

Mixed to poor performance (29.4%) was found in non-English contexts (e.g., Bahasa Malaysia F1=0.66) and with multimodal models, highlighting generalization challenges.

Ethical & Methodological Gaps

Data privacy was underreported (70.6%), algorithmic bias persists, and 82.4% of studies are from high-income countries, limiting generalizability to low-resource settings.

Implications & Recommendations

NLP has great potential, but successful integration requires robust external validation, diverse datasets, and rigorous Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) to ensure relevance and equity.

Manuscripts & Methods

Detailed Methodology & Validation Workflow

Thesis Methodology Overview

Aim 1: Build & Validate the LLM + RAG Pipeline

Data & Frameworks

NANDA, NIC, NOC, DSM-V; Public depression & suicide dialogue dataset.

Methods & Tools

RAG (Elasticsearch + dense embeddings); LLM API (e.g., GPT-4) with SCANNING-driven prompts; Optional Speech I/O.

Evaluation & Metrics

Retrieval Accuracy (Precision@k, Recall@k); Diagnostic F1-score & AUC.

Aim 2: Establish Gold Standard via Expert Review

Data & Frameworks

Pre-chat self-reports (GAD-7, PHQ-9, etc.); Venti interaction transcripts.

Methods & Tools

Two psych-nursing experts independently code transcripts; Adjudication meeting.

Evaluation & Metrics

Inter-rater reliability (Cohen’s κ); Sensitivity, specificity, κ vs. self-reports.

Aim 3: Assess Usability & Therapeutic Experience

Data & Frameworks

CUQ (Chatbot Usability Questionnaire); Interaction transcripts for qualitative analysis.

Methods & Tools

Quantitative: CUQ score analysis (descriptive + regression in R); Qualitative: Interaction Process Analysis (IPA).

Evaluation & Metrics

CUQ: mean ± SD; regression; Frequency of top 3–5 empathy-structure themes from IPA.

Population & Sampling Method

Location & Technique

The study will be conducted in the community fostered by FIK UI & the Public Health Center in Batutulis, Bogor, using Convenience Sampling.

Target Population & Limitations

Adults (21-59 years) with Diabetes Mellitus (DM), Hypertension (HT), or both. The study does not include other chronic diseases like cancer.

Validation Workflow

1

Pre-Interaction

Participants complete GAD-7, PHQ-9, SRQ-20, C-SSRS.

2

Chat Session

Interaction with Venti. Full transcript is captured and anonymized.

3

Expert Review

Two experts code for risks. Cohen’s κ is calculated for consistency, then adjudicated for a "gold standard."

4

Performance Analysis

Compute F1 & AUC of Venti’s flags against the gold-standard diagnosis. Cross-validate against self-report scores (Spearman’s ρ).

5

Usability & Experience

Aggregate CUQ scores; run IPA on transcripts to surface care-structure themes.

Theoretical Frameworks

Underpinning the Concept of Compassionate Skepticism in AI

Hunt–Vitell Ethical Model on AI (Ferrell & Ferrell, 2021)

Emphasizes moral awareness, judgment, and intent phases, adapted for AI to merge design empathy with rational control over machine decisions.

Trust as Reliance + "Extra Factor" (Durán & Formanek, 2021)

Defines trust in AI as a combination of system reliability (reliance) and moral commitment (extra factor), supporting "benefit of the doubt" and "cross-checking".

Ethical Framework (Ashok et al, 2022.)

Presents a four-domain ontology (physical, cognitive, information, governance) to map and balance ethical implications like fairness, autonomy, and privacy.

AI Trust Framework

Uses information theory to evaluate AI transparency and emphasizes audit, validation, and human-in-the-loop as forms of systematic cross-checking.

Abstract Highlights

Background

Patients with chronic illness face elevated mental health risks; scalable, holistic tools are needed.

Objective

To develop and evaluate Venti, an AI chatbot grounded in nursing epistemology.

Outcomes

High diagnostic accuracy (AUC > 0.85), strong usability (CUQ), and rich thematic analysis of empathy.

Conclusion

Successfully validates a new nursing-centric AI model for scalable, ethical mental health screening.

Contribution

A validated, nursing-centric AI model for scalable, ethical mental health screening.

Anticipated Impact of the Study

Projecting the benefits of our study for practice, science, and society.

Clinical Impact

Empowers nurses with an accurate screening tool, potentially reducing diagnostic errors and improving early detection. Increases patient access to preliminary mental health assessment, especially in resource-limited settings.

Scientific & Technological Impact

Provides a validated, nursing-informed AI model for a low-resource, non-English context. Contributes a unique dataset of Indonesian patient-chatbot interactions for future research on cultural and linguistic nuances in AI.

Community & Policy Impact

Offers a scalable solution to address gaps in community mental health services. The findings can inform public health policies on integrating AI tools to improve healthcare equity and reduce the burden on primary care facilities.
